Google leverages its AI, mapping, and cloud technologies to provide a variety of sustainable software solutions designed to reduce emissions, expand clean energy, and build climate resilience for individuals, businesses, and cities.
Consumer & City-Level Emissions Reduction:
Fuel-Efficient Routing in Google Maps: This AI-powered feature suggests driving routes that minimize fuel or energy consumption based on the vehicle's engine type and real-time traffic conditions
. In 2024, it enabled over 2.7 million metric tons of greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions reductions, which is roughly equivalent to taking 630,000 gasoline-powered cars off the road for a year
.
Green Light: An AI-based traffic optimization tool that helps city traffic engineers adjust traffic light timing to reduce stop-and-go traffic
. Implementations have shown it can reduce stops at intersections by up to 30% and lower intersection emissions by an average of over 10%
.
Nest Thermostats: Using machine learning, these devices automatically adjust home temperatures based on user habits. In 2024 alone, they saved customers more than 25 billion kWh of energy, enabling approximately 8.4 million metric tons of GHG emissions reductions
.
Clean Energy Expansion Tools:
Solar API and Project Sunroof: Google has mapped the solar potential of nearly 540 million buildings globally to help homeowners, solar installers, and city planners assess rooftop solar viability using 3D models, satellite imagery, and AI
.
Google Earth Pro: This platform assists solar and wind energy developers in evaluating potential sites and streamlining construction designs by overlaying critical datasets like topography, habitats, and flood plains
.
Enterprise Cloud and Emissions Tracking:
Google Cloud Carbon Sense Suite: A suite of tools enabling enterprise organizations to accurately measure, report, and reduce their cloud computing carbon footprint
.
Workspace and Ads Carbon Footprints: Google provides specific reporting tools for its customers to track and manage the environmental impact associated with using Google Workspace applications and Google advertising products
.
Climate Resilience and Disaster Response:
Google Flood Hub: An AI-powered forecasting system that predicts riverine floods up to seven days in advance across over 100 countries, helping at-risk populations take early anticipatory action
.
FireSat and Wildfire Mapping: Google supports FireSat, an upcoming satellite constellation utilizing AI to detect wildfires as small as a garage (5x5 meters) in near real-time
. Google also provides AI-powered wildfire boundary tracking directly in Search and Maps to deliver crisis alerts to users
.
WeatherNext: A family of AI models from Google DeepMind and Google Research that produces highly accurate weather and climate predictions up to 10 to 15 days in advance, aiding in global disaster response and grid reliability
.
Industry & Ecosystem Monitoring Platforms:
Aviation Contrail Avoidance: Google partnered with airlines to develop AI models that predict contrail-forming atmospheric zones. In trials, pilots adjusting their flight paths based on these predictions achieved a 54% reduction in contrails, which currently account for about 35% of aviation's global warming impact
.
Global Ecosystem Tracking: Google powers analytical tools like Global Fishing Watch (using AI to track over a million vessels in near real-time to monitor marine infrastructure and prevent illegal fishing) and Google Earth Engine (partnering with organizations to map deforestation drivers like logging and agriculture down to a one-kilometer scale)
